## Driver pass — semantics

### definition_short
Spatial support for gatherings of seven or more (events, lectures, ceremonies).

### definition_long
The configurational and furnishing support a setting offers for the assembly of seven-plus persons in a single bounded co-presence event — lectures, ceremonies, performances, town meetings, banquets, receptions, and rites of focused gathering. The construct measures *capacity-and-configuration affordance* for assembly: total seat or standing-room count; an open floor area large enough to hold the group without crowding (Hall's public-distance threshold of roughly 3.7 m, Hall, 1966, ch. 11); and the configurational properties that let the group function as a single gathering rather than fragmenting into parallel encounters (Goffman, 1963, pp. 18–24, on focused gatherings; Hillier & Hanson, 1984, on global connectivity). L51 is the affordance for *being-an-assembly*; it is silent on whether the assembly is presenter-directed (that is L52).

### examples_positive (≥ 6)
1. A university lecture hall with 200 fixed tiered seats, a clear floor at the front, and aisle access for entering and exiting the seated mass.
2. A church nave with rows of pews running the length of the building, total seating well above seven, with an unobstructed central aisle and a focal end wall.
3. A municipal town-hall room with stackable chairs arranged in twelve rows of fifteen, a modest stage at one end, and rear standing room.
4. A hotel banquet hall set with twenty round tables of ten, no fixed stage, dance floor in the centre — high assembly capacity, low presentation directionality.
5. A wedding-ceremony tent with rows of folding chairs facing an arch, capacity for roughly one hundred guests, a wide central aisle.
6. An empty conference ballroom whose visible square footage and lighting grid clearly imply a several-hundred-person seated capacity even without chairs deployed.
7. A community-centre multipurpose room with chairs stacked along the walls and a clear floor area large enough that, were the chairs deployed, it would seat several dozen.

### examples_negative (≥ 4)
1. A small seminar room seating twelve around a central table — the room hosts a *gathering*, but the configuration is conversational (high L44 / L48), not assembly-scale.
2. A long restaurant arranged as a sequence of four-tops along the wall — high total seat count distributed across many *separate* gatherings, not a single assembly.
3. A modern open-plan office with rows of desks — rows of seating present, but the configuration supports parallel work, not co-present assembly (focal wall and stage absent; sightlines blocked by monitors).
4. A theatre lobby — large open floor and high capacity, but used for *transit and mingling* between assemblies (high L56), not for assembly itself.
5. An auditorium photographed from a single corner of the rear, where the back wall and a few seat-rows are visible but the global capacity cannot be inferred — the cues for L51 are present but the *scene* does not establish the construct (a Hillier-style requires-floor-plan failure).

### scope_includes (≥ 3)
- Religious congregational spaces (naves, prayer halls, temple courtyards) where the assembly is the primary social use.
- Banquet and reception halls where assembly capacity exists without a presenter axis (mixed focused/unfocused).
- Outdoor amphitheatres and bleachered fields when treated as bounded assembly settings with identifiable seating or standing capacity.
- Multipurpose rooms whose *deployable* capacity is high even if chairs are currently stacked, provided the floor area and lighting/HVAC infrastructure clearly imply the supported assembly size.

### scope_excludes (≥ 3)
- Spaces that *contain* seven or more people but as a sum of parallel small gatherings (restaurants, food courts, open-plan offices) — these score on L56 (mingling) or L50 (small-group conversation), not L51.
- Pure circulation spaces (lobbies, corridors, plazas at rest) where high capacity exists but is for transit, not for assembly as a focused or mixed gathering.
- Spaces whose total area would in principle hold seven-plus persons but whose configurational properties (low ceilings, blocked sightlines, fragmented sub-rooms) prevent functioning as a single gathering — these are at most marginal L51.
- Presenter-directionality affordance — that is L52. A banquet hall with no stage is high-L51 / low-L52; a small TED-style stage with thirty raked seats is high-L52 with only modest L51.

### extraction_notes_2d
From 2D imagery, L51 should be computed as a composite over four upstream observable streams. (1) Furniture-class enumeration: upstream tag `affordance.seating_count` (from a chair / pew / bench / row-detector segmentation pass) supplies the raw seat tally; thresholds at 7, 20, 60, 200 seats anchor the four ordinal levels. (2) Open-floor area: upstream tag `affordance.isovist_area` and a derivable `affordance.open_floor_polygon_m2` together estimate the unobstructed floor available for standing assembly (Hall public-distance threshold 3.7 m as the unit cell). (3) Focal-wall / stage detection: upstream tag `affordance.focal_wall_present` (boolean) and `affordance.stage_height_estimate` modulate the *kind* of assembly afforded but only weakly the *capacity* score (kept distinct from L52). (4) Configurational connectivity: a global aisle-and-seating-row connectivity measure derived from the floor-plan augmentation (see `requires_floor_plan: true`) — without this, a single image of one corner of a large hall cannot distinguish a 30-seat conference room from a 300-seat ballroom (Hillier & Hanson, 1984). The Tier 1 designation in V2.6 is honoured only on the assumption that the 2D image is a wide-angle or panoramic view that captures the global geometry; narrow-FOV images of an assembly space should be flagged with low confidence by the eventual extractor.

### extraction_notes_3d
From 3D / VR / floor-plan substrates, L51 is straightforwardly extractable. (1) Seat-instance count is exact via furniture-instance segmentation in the mesh or scene graph. (2) Open-floor polygon is computed by projecting the unoccupied ground plane and subtracting the convex hulls of furniture footprints — this directly yields the standing-assembly capacity. (3) Stage / focal-wall is detected by elevation discontinuity (raised platform) plus a directional convergence of seating-row normals. (4) Crucially, the configurational measure `connectivity` (Hillier & Hanson, 1984; Hillier, 1996) is computed over the floor-plan adjacency graph — a single highly-connected room polygon supports a single assembly, whereas a partitioned floor of similar total area does not. The 3D substrate makes the requires_floor_plan condition trivial to satisfy; it is the 2D path that must be augmented with separately-supplied floor plans. Confidence intervals should reflect the ratio of furniture-class evidence to global-configuration evidence: extractors operating on 3D should report high confidence; extractors operating on 2D alone (without floor-plan augmentation) should cap confidence at the ordinal-level uncertainty implied by the missing global view.

## Notes

### interaction_mode
mixed

Assemblies span both focused and unfocused modes: a lecture is focused (audience attends to a presenter), a reception in the same hall is unfocused (parallel small encounters under shared co-presence), and a wedding banquet alternates between the two within a single event. L51 is the affordance for the *capacity* condition that makes either mode possible at scale; the focused/unfocused split per se is captured by L52 (presentation) and L56 (mingling) respectively.

### cross_cultural_variance
medium

The seven-person threshold and the assembly-as-bounded-co-presence concept are broadly cross-culturally recognisable (religious congregation, civic assembly, communal meal). However, the *furniture-class evidence* on which the 2D extractor leans (rows of chairs, fixed pews, raised stage) is culturally indexed: traditional Japanese assembly spaces (e.g., temple halls with floor seating), open-plan Mediterranean piazzas used for civic assembly, and African palaver-tree gatherings will all under-score on a row-of-chairs detector despite supporting the construct. Not flagged `high` (which the panel reserved for L43, L44, L48, L49, L55, where the variance is constitutive of the construct), but downstream model trainers should be aware.

### extraction_notes
No V2.6 Tier override. V2.6 Tier 1 is preserved on the assumption that wide-angle 2D imagery plus floor-plan augmentation (the panel-mandated `requires_floor_plan: true`) jointly suffice. Narrow-FOV 2D images of large assembly spaces are a known failure mode and should be flagged by the eventual extractor's confidence interval. The `configurational_measure: connectivity` setting (panel-mandated) operationalises the Hillier global-property requirement: aisle-and-row adjacency in the floor plan is the connectivity graph the extractor must compute over.
