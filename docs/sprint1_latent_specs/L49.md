## Driver pass — semantics

### definition_short

Spatial support for a focused 3–6 person F-formation, standing or seated.

### definition_long

The property of a local spatial pocket whose furniture clustering, free-floor geometry, and ambient conditions afford the formation and maintenance of a focused conversational gathering of roughly three to six co-present persons — Steiner's (1972) small-group band, in which mutual perceptual access remains tractable without sub-grouping. The construct refers to a *cluster-scale* F-formation arrangement in Kendon's (1990, ch. 7) sense: a shared o-space large enough to seat or stand-circle three to six people at conversational distance, enclosed by clusterable furniture (movable chairs, round tables of suitable diameter, an open floor pocket), and acoustically tractable enough that ordinary speech across the o-space remains intelligible. L49 is distinct from L48 (Dyadic Intimacy), which is a 2-person F-formation construct, and from L51 (Group-Assembly Affordance), which addresses 7+ gatherings whose geometry must distribute people into sub-groups; the 3–6 band is the size at which one o-space still suffices and one conversation can still be sustained (Hall, 1966, ch. 10; Steiner, 1972, ch. 4).

### examples_positive (≥ 6)

1. A round café table about 110 cm in diameter ringed by four movable chairs in a coffee-shop alcove; chair backs face outward, chair fronts converge on the table, and the surrounding open floor is wide enough for a fifth person to pull a chair up.
2. A clearing in a hotel-lobby furniture plan with a small low coffee table and five lounge chairs roughly evenly spaced around it at conversational distance, with no other furniture obstructing the cluster.
3. A standing huddle pocket in an exhibition hall where a high cocktail table is surrounded by an open floor disk roughly 2.5 m across, allowing four to six adults to stand-circle the table without bumping passers-by.
4. A meeting-room "informal corner" with three movable tub chairs and a sofa seat for one more, all converging on a small round table; the corner is set off from the main meeting table and supports a 3–4 person side-discussion.
5. A library group-study nook with a round table seating up to six, movable chairs, glass walls for acoustic separation, and an open pocket of floor that lets group members swap seats without disturbing the cluster.
6. A picnic ground arrangement of one round picnic table seating six, with the surrounding lawn open enough that group members can stand and lean during conversation while remaining in the same o-space.
7. A workplace "team huddle" zone with a round high-top table for four to six standing people, surrounded by clear floor and acoustically buffered from the open-plan desks beyond.

### examples_negative (≥ 4)

1. A long rectangular boardroom table seating twelve, with conference chairs along both sides — the geometry is sized for a single 7+ gathering with formal facing-across, not for a 3–6 person F-formation; this is a presentation/large-group configuration (L51 / L52), not L49.
2. A pair of armchairs angled inward across a small coffee table in an alcove — the cluster is sized only for two; this is L48 (Dyadic Intimacy), not L49.
3. A row of single fixed seats along a corridor wall, all facing the same direction — there is no clusterable floor pocket and no convergent o-space; this is at most a peripheral-engagement / waiting-area arrangement, not L49.
4. A theatre-style seating block with five chairs in a single row, all facing forward — the seat count is right but the geometry is parallel-rank with no o-space; this is L52 (presentation), not L49.
5. A loud open-plan office break area where four chairs sit around a small table but the ambient noise from adjacent desks and equipment makes ordinary conversation across the table impossible — the geometry says L49 but the acoustic-tractability criterion fails; classify as `none` with a low secondary affordance score and an acoustic-context note.
6. A bar with seven movable stools spaced along a straight counter-front; the seats are clusterable in count, but the linear-rank geometry and the wide spacing do not enclose an o-space for 3–6 people, so the construct does not apply.

### scope_includes (≥ 3)

- Multi-element clusters (chairs, round table, standing-table, soft seating mix) whose combined enclosed o-space is sized to seat or stand-circle 3 to 6 persons at conversational distance (Hall's (1966) personal-far to social-close band, roughly 1.2–2.5 m centre-to-centre).
- Standing-only huddle arrangements (no seats, only a high table or nothing at all) provided the surrounding floor pocket is open, free of through-traffic, and acoustically buffered enough for a stand-circle to form.
- Mixed-furniture clusters (e.g., one sofa segment plus three pull-up chairs) where the combined seat count falls in the 3–6 band and the chair fronts converge on a shared table or floor centroid.
- Round-table arrangements at the upper boundary (six seats) where a single shared conversation can plausibly be sustained without splitting into sub-groups; the upper edge is fuzzy and depends on table diameter and local acoustics.

### scope_excludes (≥ 3)

- Dyadic clusters (two seats only, however well-arranged) — these belong to L48 (Dyadic Intimacy).
- Large-group arrangements sized for 7+ persons whose geometry forces sub-grouping into multiple simultaneous conversations — these belong to L51 (Group Assembly Affordance).
- Single-cluster geometry that meets the 3–6 size criterion but sits in an environment where the ambient noise floor exceeds normal-speech intelligibility across the o-space (the V2.6 "not too loud-looking" cue) — record as `none` with a noted acoustic-tractability failure rather than a positive `circular`/`semicircular` value.
- One-to-many presentation arrangements where 3–6 chairs all face one focal speaker (lectern, screen, performer) rather than converging on a shared inner o-space — these are L52 (presentation).
- Sociofugal back-to-back or radial-out clusters whose seats are within range numerically (3–6) but whose front-normals diverge — Osmond's (1957) sociofugal pole, the inverse of the construct, recorded as `none` with secondary score 0.

### factor_associations

- V2.6 Latent_Indicators sheet, factor cluster F-CONV-3 (Small-Group Conversation Affordance): rating items "the space supports a small group talking", "movable chairs around a round table are present", "the floor pocket is open enough for 3–6 people to gather"; behavioral proxy "if 3–6 people stood/sat here, they could carry on one conversation".
- Loads on the V2.6 *interactional-affordance* superfactor alongside L44 (Sociopetal Seating Topology), L48 (Dyadic Intimacy), and L51 (Group-Assembly Affordance), with L49 occupying the 3–6 person mid-band specifically.

### related_tags (≥ 2 siblings from L41–L58)

- `social.dyadic_intimacy` (L48) — the 2-person lower-bound counterpart; L49 begins where L48 ends.
- `social.sociopetal_seating` (L44) — overlapping construct on chair-cluster observables; L44 is the seating-substrate angular-convergence property in general, while L49 specifically gates on the 3–6 band size and adds free-floor / acoustic-tractability requirements.
- `social.group_assembly_affordance` (L51) — the 7+ upper-bound counterpart; L49 ends where L51 begins.
- `social.one_to_many_presentation` (L52) — the explicit non-F-formation alternative for 3–6 seats arranged in parallel-rank facing a focal speaker rather than converging on a shared o-space.

### inverse_of

null. The construct's failure mode is heterogeneous (too few seats / too many seats / wrong geometry / acoustic failure / single-focus-rank), so a single named inverse construct is not appropriate; the `none` enum value covers all failure modes.

## Engineer pass — extractability, method, BN

### value_type assignment

- `value_type`: `categorical`
- `value_range`: enum `[vis_a_vis, l_arrangement, side_by_side, semicircular, circular, none]`
- `unit`: `enum`
- `secondary_value_type`: `ordinal`
- `secondary_value_range`: `[0, 4]`
- `secondary_unit`: `likert_0_4`

The categorical primary value names the geometric *kind* of small-group F-formation arrangement implied by the cluster (Kendon, 1990, ch. 7). For L49 specifically, the `circular` and `semicircular` values are the modal positives — small groups of 3–6 typically form either a full ring (around a round table) or an arc (lounge chairs around a hearth or coffee table). `vis_a_vis` and `l_arrangement` are admissible at the lower edge of the size band (3 people) and `side_by_side` is admissible only with a shared focal artefact (e.g., 3–4 people on a sofa facing a coffee table). The secondary ordinal carries the *strength of affordance* judgment, integrating size-fit (does the cluster genuinely accommodate 3–6 at conversational distance?), free-floor adequacy (can a fifth or sixth person join without disturbing the formation?), and acoustic-context tractability (the V2.6 "not too loud-looking" cue, operationalised against the available ambient-noise observable).

### extraction_notes_2d

The extractor consumes upstream observable tags — most centrally `furniture.chair_cluster_count` (number of chairs spatially grouped within an inter-chair distance threshold), `furniture.round_table_present` (round-table detection within the cluster), `furniture.chair_orientation` (per-chair front-normal in image plane), `geometry.free_floor_pocket_radius` (distance from cluster centroid to the nearest occlusion or through-traffic boundary), `geometry.cluster_distance_estimate` (Hall-zone distance estimate between cluster members; mandated by Hall-panel disposition §3 viii because without metric, L49 collapses into L51 — a circle of chairs at social-close distance (4–7 ft / 1.2–2.1 m centres) supports L49 conversation, while the same chairs at social-far (7–12 ft / 2.1–3.7 m) supports a seminar (L51 / L52)), and `scene.ambient_noise_proxy` (a visual proxy for the acoustic context: presence of acoustic baffling, distance from open kitchen / equipment, glass-wall enclosure, etc.) for the V2.6 "not too loud-looking" cue. The extractor MUST emit, alongside the categorical and secondary-Likert values, the F-formation-geometry sub-field `(o_space_centroid_xy, o_space_radius_px, predominant_arrangement)` per Kendon-panel mandate (panel disposition §3 ix; Kendon, 1990, pp. 209–214). Cluster-size gating: the categorical value defaults to `none` when the chair-cluster-count is <3 (cede to L48) or >6 (cede to L51); within the 3–6 band, the `predominant_arrangement` is computed from the same angular-threshold logic as L44 (vis-à-vis at ~180° front-normal, L at ~90°, side-by-side at ~0° with shared focal artefact, semicircular at chair-back arc <180° around the centroid, circular at arc ≥270°). **Cross-cultural-variance warning (Hall, 1966, ch. 10)**: this extractor is calibrated against Western indoor-furniture corpora; it will systematically misread (a) Japanese tatami-room small-group arrangements where seating is around a low shared table at distances Western thresholds may flag as too close, (b) Mediterranean / Latin-American small-group conversational distances which the Hall-zone gate may push down a Likert step, and (c) Middle-Eastern majlis-style perimeter seating where the ring is technically circular but at distances exceeding the o-space radius constraint. Downstream model trainers MUST treat L49 outputs as culturally indexed and the secondary Likert as conditionally interpretable on the cultural prior of the scene.

### extraction_notes_3d

In a 3D / VR substrate the extraction is materially easier: chair instances and orientations are metric, the floor plane is given, and the free-floor pocket can be computed as the largest empty disk centred on the cluster centroid that contains no other furniture or wall surface up to 0.9 m height. The extractor should take the 3D scene graph, identify chair clusters whose pairwise floor-plane distance falls within the Hall personal-far / social-close band (1.2–2.5 m centre-to-centre — the metric that distinguishes L49 from L51 per Hall, 1966, ch. 10, and per the panel's special note that without distance estimation L49 collapses into L51), gate on cluster size 3 ≤ n ≤ 6, project chair-front normals onto the floor plane, and compute the o-space centroid as the smallest enclosed region whose boundary contains all chair-front rays within their first 2.5 m. The arrangement-enum classification applies the same angular thresholds as in 2D but with metric distance gating: a circle of six chairs at 1.4 m centres is `circular` with secondary score 4; the same chairs at 3 m centres steps down to secondary 2 (and at 4 m centres steps to `none` because the cluster has crossed into seminar-scale L51 territory). Acoustic context is more directly estimable in 3D from room volume, surface materials, and distance to noise sources; the secondary score should integrate a simple acoustic-tractability boolean from these. As with L44 and L48, occupancy state is not part of L49 — the construct is an *affordance* of the geometry alone, with avatar occupancy contributing only to a separate confidence-of-realisation field. The `requires_floor_plan: false` setting holds even in 3D: L49 is a local cluster-pocket property, not a configurational property of the room as a whole.

All overlap fractions sit safely below 50%. The Phase D `audit-extraction-plan` validator should confirm.

## Notes

### interaction_mode

`focused`. Small-group conversation support is constitutively an affordance for a *focused encounter* in Goffman's (1963) sense — a sustained mutual involvement around a shared concern, with the o-space marking the boundary of the involvement. The construct does not apply to unfocused-encounter spaces (waiting areas, transit halls), and cluster geometry that affords only intermittent or peripheral engagement is recorded under L45 / L58 rather than L49.

### cross_cultural_variance

`high`. Hall (1966, ch. 10) documents marked differences across North American, Mediterranean, Japanese, and Middle-Eastern small-group conversational arrangements: distances vary by roughly a factor of two between Northern-European and Mediterranean conventions, side-by-side substitutes for face-to-face in Japanese small-group settings, and majlis-style perimeter rings exceed the Western o-space radius constraint. An extractor calibrated on Western indoor-furniture corpora will systematically mis-classify these. See `extraction_notes_2d` for the explicit Western-calibration warning.

### extraction_notes

No V2.6 Tier override applied — L49 remains Tier 1 as in V2.6. Three extractor-contract requirements deserve special note for the Phase E stub author: (a) the F-formation-geometry sub-field `(o_space_centroid_xy, o_space_radius_px, predominant_arrangement)` is mandatory output (Kendon panel mandate, panel disposition §3 ix); (b) the Hall distance-zone estimate per Hall (1966) is mandatory output (Hall panel mandate, panel disposition §3 viii) — and for L49 this is doubly load-bearing because without metric the construct collapses into L51 per the panel's special note for this latent; (c) the acoustic-tractability proxy (the V2.6 "not too loud-looking" cue) should be emitted as a separate boolean alongside the secondary Likert score so that downstream consumers can re-weight or veto on acoustic grounds. All three are runtime extractor-output specifications, not registry-level fields, and so are recorded here rather than in `extractability`.
